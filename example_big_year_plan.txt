Big year 2026
The backbone
Every week (non-negotiable):
2 local patch sessions (45–90 min, dawn)


1 longer outing (2–5 hrs) rotating habitat


Log everything in eBird; keep a running “miss list”


Every month:
1 day trip (or occasional overnight) to a high-yield hotspot within 5 hours



Target milestones (cumulative species)
End Feb: 90–120


End Apr: 150–185


End Jun: 210–245


End Jul: 240–270


End Aug (MN+WA): 290–330


End Nov: 300+ locked



Month-by-month schedule (with 5-hour trips)
Jan (Winter waterfowl + raptors)
Goal: +60–90
 Day trip: Carson Valley + Washoe Lake (easy ducks/raptors)
 Optional upgrade: Stillwater NWR / Fallon (huge duck/diver variety)
Feb (Gulls + more waterfowl)
Goal: +15–25
 Day trip: Sacramento National Wildlife Refuge complex (5 hrs)
 This is one of your best “species per day” trips all year.
Mar (Early migrants + raptor movement)
Goal: +20–35
 Day trip: Sierra Valley (near Loyalton/Sierraville) + Feather River canyon edges
 Wet meadows + open country = cranes, swans (sometimes), raptors, early songbirds.
Apr (Migration ramps up)
Goal: +25–40
 Day trip: Yolo Bypass Wildlife Area / Davis wetlands (great mix: shorebirds, ducks, passerines)
 If you want variety, add a quick stop at a riparian corridor in the valley.
May (Peak passerine migration + foothills)
Goal: +35–60 (biggest month locally + nearby)
 Day trip: American River Parkway (Sacramento) or Putah Creek style riparian
 This is where you pile up warblers, vireos, flycatchers, orioles.
Jun (Breeding specialties: alpine + Great Basin)
Goal: +20–35
 Day trip: Mono Lake + Lee Vining + Bodie area (within 5 hrs)
 Adds: alkali lake birds, sagebrush birds, high desert breeders.
Jul (Shorebirds start + high desert + owls)
Goal: +15–25
 Day trip: Honey Lake / Susanville area (NE CA wetlands + sage)
 One night session: owling locally (you’ll pick up species you otherwise miss).
Aug (MN + WA travel = massive boost)
Goal: +40–80 depending on effort
 In your 5-hour radius, do one coastal day if possible either before or after your trip:
Point Reyes / Bolinas / Tomales Bay (marine + shorebird injection)
 Even one day on the coast can add 15–30 species you won’t get at Tahoe.


Sep (Fall migration + shorebirds)
Goal: +20–35
 Day trip: Suisun Marsh / Delta (near Fairfield/Antioch area)
 This is a fall goldmine for shorebirds, rails, ducks, raptors, and passerines.
Oct (Waterfowl return + raptors)
Goal: +15–30
 Day trip: Sacramento NWR again (different set now)
 Or Cosumnes River Preserve (cranes/geese season ramping).
Nov (Winter lock-in month)
Goal: +10–20
 Day trip: Point Reyes or Sacramento Valley depending on what you’re missing:
Need gulls/sea ducks? go coast/bay


Need ducks/geese/cranes? go valley refuges


Dec (Cleanup + rarity chasing within 5 hours)
Goal: finish 300+
 Do one targeted chase based on what you’re short on (often gulls, shorebirds, or certain sparrows/finches).

The 6 “big yield” destinations within ~5 hours
These are your core rotation when you want to add species fast:
Sacramento NWR complex (winter/fall)


Yolo Bypass / Davis wetlands (spring/fall)


American River Parkway / valley riparian (April–May)


Mono Lake / Lee Vining / Bodie (June–July)


Honey Lake / Susanville wetlands (summer/fall)


Point Reyes / Tomales Bay (anytime; best for coastal specialties)


Do 4–6 of these over the year and 300 becomes very realistic.

What to do in Minnesota + Washington (to maximize species)
Since those trips are already happening, treat them like “boost weeks”:
2 early-morning sessions in each state


1 big habitat day in each state


MN: wetlands + prairie edge


WA: if you can do any saltwater/estuary day, do it



A simple “species gap” rule
If you’re behind pace, don’t panic—just choose the right habitat:
Missing ducks/geese/cranes → Sacramento refuges


Missing shorebirds/gulls → Delta / coast


Missing sagebrush/desert birds → Mono Basin / Honey Lake


Missing warblers/spring songbirds → valley riparian in May
